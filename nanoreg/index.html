How to acknowledge
A good rule of thumb is to use the acronym TASL, which stands for Title, Author, Source, License.
  • Title - What is the name of the material?
  • Author - Who owns the material? In case of a deliverable the lead beneficiary (organisation) should be mentioned. In case of Factsheets and templates, NANoREG (including a link to the NANoREG Result Repository) should be mentioned
  • Source - Where can I find it? You can provide a link to the document in the NANoREG Results Repository or in case of experimental data, the NANoREG - eNanoMapper database
  • License - How can I use it?

Examples:
  • Citing Experimental data
    • Experimental nanoEHS; NANoREG - eNanoMapper database; CC BY-NC-SA 4.0
  • Citing deliverables
    • NANoREG DR [Dx.yy} [year] [acronym lead beneficiary] et al [if applicable] CC BY-NC-SA 4.0
  • Citing SOPs
    • NANoREG SOP [Name of the SOP] [year] [acronym lead beneficiary] et al [if applicable] CC BY-NC-SA 4.0
Highlighted green: include a link to the document
Highlighted yellow: The project office will insert a link to the data base or list of partners

(non) Commercial Use

The NANoREG - eNanoMapper database and NANoREG deliverables, SOPs and other documents [hereinafter: sources] may be used by individuals, institutions, governments, corporations or other business entities so long as the use itself or any works derived from the use of these sources, are not intended to generate sales or profit.
This means you cannot:

  • Directly sell or profit from these sources or from works derived from the sources
  • Use the sources internally for commercial product development
  • Use the NANoREG - eNanoMapper data base as a component of a product or within software to be made commercially available.
If you wish to use the resources for commercial purposes, please contact in case of written documents, the lead beneficiary mentioned in the document and in case of the NANoREG - eNanoMapper database the NANoREG coordinator pobox@nanoreg.eu
